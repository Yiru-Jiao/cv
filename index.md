I'm a passionate researcher working for autonomous road safety. My research aims at safer traffic for all road users utilising automated technologies, which include but are not limited to autonomous driving, decentralized risk management, and data-driven improvement of mobility systems. 

I have a mixed education background in Operations Research and Management Information Systems; self-taught knowledge in Data Science, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Reinforcement Learning; as well as solution-driven skills in Artificial Intelligence by addressing theoretical and practical problems. 

My scientific interests are generally in collective patterns emerged in individual interactions, of which the curiousity motivated my pursuit of a doctoral degree. Supported by [<u>Delft AI Labs & Talent Programme</u>](https://www.tudelft.nl/ai/tu-delft-ai-labs), I obtained my doctorate with Cum Laude (less than 4%) at Delft University of Technology, and my thesis is [<u>Proactive Collision Risk Quantification in Multi-directional Traffic Interactions</u>](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/proactive-collision-risk-quantification-in-multi-directional-traf/). Most of my papers published during PhD are openly accessible thanks to the TU Delft Library. For every paper, code for experiments and instructions to reproduce the results are open-sourced in GitHub.

This digital CV is very bibliometrically formed for HR's or quantitative eyes. You are invited to visit my [GitHub page](https://github.com/Yiru-Jiao) and use a qualitative view to look at me and my research. I believe all researchers in academia would ask the same. We work for a better world, not for producing publications.
